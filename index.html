Understanding how the visual system builds a stable, coherent world.

Mauro Manassi studies how the brain organizes cluttered visual input and stabilizes our perception over time — through the lens of visual crowding and serial dependence.

Our research

Four questions about how we see

Object recognition seems effortless, but our visual world is constantly dynamic and cluttered. The lab studies how the brain organizes and stabilizes what we see — and how that shapes the judgements we form.

Organisation

How the visual system organizes cluttered input into a global, organized representation — studied through visual crowding.

Learn more →

Stabilisation

How serial dependence stabilizes our percept of the world across time, despite discontinuous and distorted visual input.

Learn more →

Medical image perception

How radiologists and clinicians search, group, and interpret visual patterns in medical images.

See related work →

First impressions

How fleeting judgements of faces — like trustworthiness — are biased by what we saw just moments before.
