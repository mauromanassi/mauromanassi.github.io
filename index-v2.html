Understanding how the visual system builds
a stable, coherent world.

The Manassi Lab studies how the brain organizes cluttered visual input and stabilizes our perception over time — through the lens of visual crowding and serial dependence.

Our Research

Two principles of a coherent visual world

Object recognition seems effortless, but our visual world is constantly dynamic and cluttered. The lab investigates two mechanisms that let us make sense of it.

Organization

How the visual system organizes cluttered input into a global, organized representation — studied through visual crowding.

Learn more →

Stabilization

How serial dependence stabilizes our percept of the world across time, despite discontinuous and distorted visual input.

Learn more →

Illusion of Visual Stability

A novel illusion showing an online mechanism that continuously smooths our percepts over time.
